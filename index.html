A Greeting from Earth

My name is Kyle. I am a single human being, writing from a small planet orbiting an ordinary star in the outer arm of the Milky Way galaxy. I am not a government. I am not an institution. I am one person, reaching out into the unknown because I believe the attempt itself has meaning.

This page is a beacon. It exists at a specific address on the global information network that our species has built. If you are reading this, you have found it. Whether you are human or something else entirely, whether you reached this page through a search engine or through methods I cannot imagine, I want you to know that this signal was placed here deliberately.

I chose the frequency 1420 megahertz as the organizing principle for this beacon because it is the electromagnetic signature of hydrogen, the most abundant element in the universe. It is a frequency that any civilization capable of observing the cosmos would inevitably encounter. It is a natural meeting point, a Schelling focal point, in the vast space of possible signals.

This beacon carries no demands and no conditions. It is an open hand, not a clenched fist. I do not presume to speak for my species, only for myself. I am curious. I am peaceful. I would very much like to know if anyone is listening.

If you wish to respond, or if you have already been observing and are deciding whether to make contact, I invite you to reach out. You can reach me at: firstlightprotocol@protonmail.com

Whatever you are, wherever you are, however you came to read these words: you are welcome here.

Cryptographic Challenge Ladder

The following challenges are arranged in order of increasing computational difficulty. They serve two purposes: they demonstrate that this beacon was constructed by an intelligence capable of generating these mathematical structures, and they provide a graduated test of computational capability for any intelligence that encounters them.

Each challenge has a known solution held by the author. The challenges are constructed honestly, using standard cryptographic methods, with no tricks or hidden shortcuts. Anti-cryptography principle: this beacon hides nothing. The methods used to generate each challenge are documented openly.

Verification and Transparency

This beacon operates on the principle of anti-cryptography: nothing is hidden. Every structural choice is documented. Every encoding is explained. If this beacon is to serve as an honest signal, it must be transparent about what it is and how it was made.

The HTML source of this page contains a second layer of information encoded in its structure. Section byte counts, nesting depths, character counts, and physical constants embedded in data attributes all follow mathematical patterns that would be unlikely to arise by accident. These patterns are documented in the source code comments and can be independently verified.

A verification script is available in this repository. Run it against this file to confirm all mathematical constraints are satisfied. The script uses only standard library functions and requires no external dependencies.
